The Philosophy


Do you care about staying in shape? Do you want to lose weight? Put on muscle? If you answered yes to any of these questions but think you have to relegate yourself to boiled chicken breast and broccoli dinners for the rest of your life, fear not! It turns out you can in fact enjoy eating out without getting fat or inhibiting your progress at the gym.

No, I am not trying to sell you snake oil or sabotage your gains. Everything presented here is well established science. You can in fact enjoy a Big Mac from McDonalds for lunch every day and still achieve all of your fitness goals. As it turns out weight loss and weight gain really is as simply as calories in and calories out. As we know from the first law of thermodynamics, energy cannot be created or destroyed but only transferred from one form to another. Our bodies require energy to be able to go through the day. Even if you're just staring up at the ceiling in silence your body is still using energy to perform all the metabolic functions that keep you alive and that energy has to come from somewhere. And where do we get our energy from? That's right. Food.


Calories are essentially a measure of how much energy is in the food you eat. Depending on your height, weight, age, physical activity level, and other factors, we all burn a certain amount of calories each day which comes from the food we eat. If you want to find out how many calories your body requires each day to maintain your current lifestyle and weight, try using this online calculator.

So knowing that we burn a certain number of calories each day and that those calories come from food, what happens if we consume fewer calories from food than we burn in that day? This brings us to the concept of a caloric deficit, the key to weight loss. You may have noticed that if you didn't eat a lot one day your body didn't just totally shut down on you. This is because our body stores fat precisely for situations like those. Fat in your body is energy in reserve that is there' as a backup plan to when food is not readily available. Again, since we know energy cannot be created or destroyed but only transferred, if you're not getting the energy you need from food to go about your day, your body will get that energy from fat storage instead. This is how weight loss (specifically fat loss) occurs. In order to consistently lose fat for any extended period of time, you MUST be in a caloric deficit meaning you consume fewer calories than your body burns off in a day forcing your body to burn fat instead.


To provide an example of how this works, let's say your daily caloric burn is 2500 calories and you only eat 2000 calories for one whole week. That means you will burn 500 calories worth of fat a day from your body's fat storage. A pound of fat is roughly equal to 3500 calories so if you burn 500 a day for 7 days a week you will burn off approximately 1 pound of fat in a week (500 x 7 = 3500).

The process for gaining weight follows the same logic. Any calories you consume that your body does not burn are stored in the body as fat. This is known as a caloric surplus. So if the same person who burns 2500 calories a day ate 3000 calories a day they would be putting on roughly 1 pound of fat per week.

What about muscle? Muscle is built by a combination of resistance training and proper nutrition, particularly proper protein intake as protein is comprised of amino acids which are the building blocks of muscle in the body. It is recommended to get between 0.65 to 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight if your goal is to put on muscle. It is generally considered easier to put on muscle in a caloric surplus than a caloric deficit or maintenance but it is not not impossible to do so. I would recommend using an online calculator to figure out exactly how much protein, as well as fats and carbohydrates (the 3 macronutrients) you should be getting as well as how many calories you should be eating. Protein, fat, and carbs are your Macros and once you know how many of each you should be getting you can start eating for your goals, whether they be weight loss, muscle gain, or just maintenance of your current body composition.

Once you figure that out, that's where this site comes in. As it turns out Big Macs and all other food items consist of calories from protein, fats, and carbs and as long as you are meeting your macronutrient and caloric requirements for your goals, you can reach them even eating fast food along the way! Now, bear in mind there are other health concerns that are worth considering such cholesterol and saturated fat content that may affect heart health. However as long as you consume "bad foods" in moderation to avoid the previously mentioned problems with cholesterol and otherwise and keep to your macro and calorie numbers you can achieve your fitness and body goals and this site is here to help you with just that.
