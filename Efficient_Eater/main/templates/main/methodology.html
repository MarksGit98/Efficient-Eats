Methodology

Efficiency is a ratio of calories from protein (other another specified macro-nutrient) over the total number of calories in the meal displayed as a percentage. For example if a meal contained 500 calories and 25 grams of protein the efficiency of the meal is calculated as such: 25 grams of protein X 4 calories per gram (there are 4 calories in each gram of protein) = 100 calories from protein. 100 calories from protein / 500 total calories = 0.2 or 20% efficiency.


There are three primary macro-nutrients, carbohydrates (carbs), fat, and protein that comprise the total caloric content of any particular item of food. Carbs and protein are both 4 calories per gram and fat is 9 calories per gram. The Efficiency ratio referenced in the "Nutrition" tab on this site is protein as this is typically the macro-nutrient most people tend to concern themselves with but efficiency ratios for all three macro-nutrients are displayed in the pie chart in both the "Lookup" tab for every item and in the "Calculator" tab for every user-inputted order.


Protein is comprised of amino acids which are used to maintain and repair muscle tissue. It is recommended to get between 0.65 grams - 1.0 gram of protein per pound of body weight a day if your goal is to build muscle. Although they do not directly build muscle it is vital to get the proper amount of carbohydrates and fat in your diet as well to maximize muscle growth and overall health.
